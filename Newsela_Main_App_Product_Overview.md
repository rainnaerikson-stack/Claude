## WHAT IT IS

Newsela is a K–12 instructional content platform built on a core premise: every student should be able to access the same texts and topics as their peers, at their own reading level. The platform delivers real-world news, literary content, primary sources, and curated collections — each adapted to multiple Lexile levels — paired with a growing AI layer called Luna.

> **◆ KEY INSIGHT** · Newsela's core differentiator is not content volume — it is Lexile-differentiated access to the same text, for every student in a classroom, simultaneously.

### Product at a Glance

| Attribute | Details |
|---|---|
| **Primary audience** | K–12 teachers (ELA, Social Studies, STEM, News literacy) |
| **Content types** | News, ELA literary texts, Social Studies, STEM, Spanish (en Español), videos, text sets, novel studies, worksheets |
| **Lexile differentiation** | Each article available at 3–5 reading levels (e.g., 560L to MAX/1300L+) |
| **AI layer** | Luna AI — search, lesson planning, CFU generation, personalization, text leveling |
| **Assignment platform** | Built-in assignment builder with activities, Formative integration, schedule controls |
| **Reports** | Classroom Data (reading level, quiz scores, time-on-task), Reading Skills (8 skills), Power Words |
| **Key integrations** | Google Classroom, Clever, Canvas LMS (class sync); Formative (interactive activities) |

The Newsela home dashboard is a curated, personalized teacher hub. It surfaces multiple content streams simultaneously — district-promoted content, AI-recommended articles, latest news, featured ELA editorial picks — alongside an actionable onboarding checklist.

## CONTENT DISCOVERY

Newsela provides three distinct pathways for teachers to find content: the global search bar (with Luna AI search mode), the Subjects navigation menu, and the Content Library mega-menu.

### Standard Search

The search bar sits in the global nav, powered by Luna. Typing any query shows a dropdown with trending topics and a "Search with Luna" toggle.

- Search returns articles, text sets, collections, novel studies, and videos
- 8,444 results for "civil rights movement" — indicates depth of the content library
- Filter panel: Grade Level (3rd–12th), Content Type, Reading Level, Subject, Standards, Language, Maturity Level
- Grade filter reduces results proportionally (8,444 → 3,010 results for 7th grade)
- Selected filters appear as removable chips at the top of results

*Screenshots: n05_search_dropdown_trending.png · n06_search_results_civil_rights.png · n07_search_filtered_7th_grade.png*

### Luna AI Search (BETA)

Toggling "Search with Luna" activates a conversational search mode. Luna accepts natural language queries and responds with a clarifying question while simultaneously surfacing relevant results.

- Natural language input: "I need articles about civil rights for 7th graders"
- Luna asks a clarifying question mid-flow (e.g., "Are you looking for events, biographies, or primary sources?")
- Luna auto-filters content types (URL changes: `content_types=ARTICLE&content_types=BUNDLE`)
- Marked as BETA — active feature in development

> **★ LUNA AI** · Luna AI Search supports natural language queries. Teachers can search with full sentences and receive clarifying questions and filtered results — a meaningfully different interaction model than keyword search with manual filter dropdowns.

## ARTICLE READER

The article reader is Newsela's core product experience. Every text is rendered in a clean layout with teacher tools in a persistent top bar, a Luna AI side panel below the article header, and an Assignment Builder on the right.

### Reading Level Differentiation

Each article is available at 3–5 Lexile levels. Teachers switch levels via a dropdown in the top bar; students may also be granted the ability to change their own level.

- Level selector shows: MAX, 960L, 810L, 650L, 560L (5 levels for the civil rights article)
- "MAX" is the original, unmodified source text
- Each level preserves the same core facts and narrative while adjusting vocabulary, sentence complexity, and length
- Language selector: English / Español — full Spanish adaptation available
- Teachers can lock students to a specific level or allow level switching

*Screenshots: n10_article_reader_luna_planner.png · n11_reading_level_selector.png*

### Article Toolbar Features

- **Share** – Generate and copy a shareable link
- **Save content** – Bookmark to "My content"
- **Translate** – Toggle paragraph-level AI translation (per assignment settings)
- **Listen** – Text-to-speech audio playback
- **Citation** – Auto-generate a formatted citation
- **View more actions** – Mobile-friendly overflow menu
- **Print options** – Teacher-controlled print/download settings

### Power Words (Vocabulary)

Key vocabulary terms are highlighted inline as "Power Words." Clicking any Power Word opens a contextual definition panel. Power Words are also an assignable activity (ELA Power Words quiz).

- Power Words are underlined and highlighted in the article body
- Clicking opens a contextual popup with definition, part of speech, and example sentence
- ELA Power Words activity: students match terms to definitions in a structured quiz

> **◆ KEY INSIGHT** · The combination of Lexile-leveled text, Power Words vocabulary, Listen audio, and paragraph-level translation gives teachers four independent accessibility levers on a single article — each configurable per assignment.

## LUNA AI ECOSYSTEM

> **★ LUNA AI** · Luna is Newsela's AI layer — integrated into search, lesson planning, content recommendations, comprehension check generation, and vocabulary supports. It surfaces within the article reader as a 5-tab "Apply to your lesson" panel.

### Tab 1 — Lesson Planner (NEW badge)

The Lesson Planner tab is the most feature-complete tab in the Luna panel. It generates a lesson plan for the article based on teacher inputs: a learning objective, a standard from the standards picker, optional supporting materials, and allotted time.

- Input: learning objective (free text), standard (searchable picker), supporting materials, time allocation
- Standards picker searches by code (e.g., "7R1") across all US state standards
- Output: CREATED BY LUNA badge with Edit / Copy to clipboard / Download as PDF actions
- Generated plan includes: Standards, Objective, Reading Instructions, Activity Ideas (6), Lesson Breakdown with timed segments
- Activity Ideas link directly to relevant Graphic Organizers
- Lesson Breakdown sample: Introduction/Hook (10 min), Pre-Reading (5 min), Guided Reading (20 min), Collaborative Activity (15 min), Independent Practice/Closure (10 min)
- AI disclaimer: "While our AI is smart, it's not perfect: please review the lesson plan before using it with your class."

*Screenshots: n12_luna_lesson_planner_form.png · n13_standards_picker_7R1_selected.png · n14_luna_lesson_plan_output.png · n15b_luna_lesson_plan_activities.png*

### Tab 2 — Main Idea

Luna generates a concise AI summary of the article's main idea — useful for pre-reading teacher prep or as a student comprehension reference.

- AI-generated summary appears in a text box with a Copy button
- Transparency disclaimer: "generated by an AI model, but the ideas and underlying gist are original and generated by a human author"
- Report content button for flagging inaccurate AI output

*Screenshot: n16_luna_main_idea.png*

### Tab 3 — Key Terms

Luna identifies and defines the most important vocabulary terms from the article in age-appropriate, context-sensitive language.

- Sample output (civil rights article): civil rights movement, spirituals, anthem, desegregate — each with part of speech and definition
- Definitions are contextualized to the article subject matter, not generic dictionary entries
- Copy button for easy transfer to lesson materials

*Screenshot: n17_luna_key_terms.png*

### Tab 4 — Before Reading Activity

Luna generates pre-reading discussion questions designed to activate prior knowledge and frame the upcoming text.

- Sample output: 3 pre-reading questions targeting prior knowledge and essential questions for the article topic
- Structured as open-ended discussion starters, not comprehension checks

*Screenshot: n23_luna_before_reading_activity.png*

### Tab 5 — Discussion Questions

Luna generates text-dependent post-reading discussion questions that require students to return to the text for evidence.

- Sample output: 3 text-dependent questions tied to article content
- Questions follow a Bloom's Taxonomy progression from recall to evaluation

> **★ LUNA AI** · The 5-tab Luna panel covers before, during, and after reading in a single location: Before Reading Activity → Lesson Planner / Main Idea / Key Terms → Discussion Questions. All five outputs are generated for the specific article being viewed, not from a generic template.

*Screenshot: n24_luna_discussion_questions.png*

## ASSIGNMENT CREATION

Newsela's assignment workflow is split into two stages: (1) the Assignment Builder, where teachers configure which activities to include, and (2) the Create Assignment dialog, where they configure Lexile level, student selection, scheduling, and accessibility settings.

### Assignment Builder — Activities

The Assignment Builder panel slides in from the right side of the article reader. It presents a categorized checklist of all assignable activities.

- **Annotations** – Students highlight and comment on the text
- **Student Poll** – Teacher-created opinion or prediction poll
- **Checks for Understanding** – Luna-generated comprehension questions embedded in the article body (checked by default)
- **Guided Highlighting** – Structured text-marking with teacher-defined categories
- **Graphic Organizers** – Pre-built organizers (Main Idea, Compare/Contrast, Know/Wonder/Learn)
- **Social Studies: Write Prompt** – Discipline-specific writing prompt
- **ELA: Quiz (2 reading skills)** – Multiple choice quiz targeting 2 of 8 ELA reading skills (checked by default)
- **ELA: Power Words** – Vocabulary matching/definition activity (checked by default)
- **ELA: Write Prompt** – ELA writing response prompt (checked by default)
- **Review Student Work** – Direct link to student submission review
- **Formative integration** – Pre-built interactive graphic organizers via Formative (e.g., 6 Connecting People/Events/Ideas, 2 Point of View & Purpose)

*Screenshots: n25_assignment_builder_top.png · n26_assignment_builder_activities.png · n27_assignment_builder_ela_formative.png · n28_assignment_builder_formative.png*

### Create Assignment Dialog — Settings

Clicking "Assign" opens a modal dialog for final assignment configuration.

- **Lexile Level** – "Newsela recommended" (auto) or teacher-specified; toggle for "Students can change level"
- **Students** – Assign to whole class or small group (requires class setup; LMS sync supported)
- **Scheduled for** – Date/time picker; assignments can be scheduled in advance
- **Students can listen to the article** – Text-to-speech toggle (on by default)
- **Students can change the entire article's language** – Language switching toggle (on by default)
- **Reveal quiz answers to students after submission** – Answer transparency control (on by default)
- **Disable AI-generated vocabulary supports** – Turn off Power Word definitions (off by default)
- **Students can translate each paragraph using AI** – Paragraph-level AI translation (on by default)
- **Save draft** / **Create assignment** – Draft or publish immediately

> **◆ KEY INSIGHT** · The assignment settings menu reveals the depth of Newsela's accessibility architecture: audio playback, language switching, paragraph translation, and vocabulary AI can all be independently controlled per assignment.

*Screenshots: n30_create_assignment_dialog.png · n31_assignment_additional_settings.png*

## STUDENT EXPERIENCE

Teachers can preview the student-facing experience via the "View as student" toggle in the article reader top bar. In student mode, the Assignment Builder transforms into a navigable Activities panel, and the article introduces comprehension gating via embedded CFU questions.

### View as Student Mode

- Assignment Builder → becomes "Activities" panel showing: Annotations, Social Studies Write Prompt, ELA Quiz, ELA Power Words, ELA Write Prompt
- Luna "Apply to your lesson" panel remains visible (teacher tool, not student-facing)
- Toggle state is clearly indicated by a filled blue toggle

*Screenshot: n29_view_as_student_activities.png*

### Check For Understanding — Reading Gates

Newsela's most distinctive student engagement feature is the inline Check for Understanding (CFU) — a Luna-generated comprehension question embedded directly in the article body. Students must answer correctly to continue reading.

- CFU appears as a distinct highlighted block at natural break points in the article
- Label: "CHECK FOR UNDERSTANDING 1 OF 2" with a LUNA tag and a lock icon
- Instruction: "Students must answer the question correctly to continue reading"
- Multiple-choice format with 4 answer options (A–D)
- Correct answer is identified with a green checkmark in teacher preview mode
- Students have unlimited tries; text remains hidden until answered correctly
- Sample article had 2 CFUs embedded at different points in the text

> **★ LUNA AI** · The reading gate (CFU) model forces comprehension at natural break points rather than treating reading as passive content consumption. This is particularly relevant for students who would otherwise skip through the text.

## REPORTS & ANALYTICS

Newsela provides teachers and administrators with three reports: Classroom Data, Power Words, and Usage Report — accessible from the top nav Reports dropdown.

### Classroom Data Report

The most detailed teacher-facing report, giving a per-student breakdown of reading activity and comprehension performance.

**Class Overview:**
- Average reading level (grade-level equivalent and average Lexile of texts viewed)
- Total texts viewed by class
- Minutes average reading time
- Texts read by grade level: Above / At / Below (counts with trend chart)

**Reading Activity table (per student):**
- Student name, Text level, Views, Active Time, Quizzes, Scores, Write prompts, Annotations, Power words

## KEY STRENGTHS

> **✓ NOTE** · The following are based on direct hands-on exploration on March 23, 2026. Claims about depth or differentiation reflect what was directly observed — not benchmarked against all competing products.

1. **Lexile differentiation is granular and wide-ranging.** Every article spans a 600+ Lexile range (e.g., 560L → MAX), allowing a classroom of diverse readers to access the same content simultaneously. This is applied consistently across the full content library — not limited to a subset of articles.

2. **Luna AI is deeply embedded — not bolted on.** Luna is integrated into search, lesson planning, comprehension check generation, vocabulary, personalization, text leveling, and recommendations. This is a platform-level AI strategy.

3. **The 5-tab Luna article panel covers the full instructional cycle.** Before Reading → Lesson Planner → Main Idea → Key Terms → Discussion Questions gives teachers everything needed to build a complete lesson without leaving the article reader.

4. **Check for Understanding gating enforces active reading.** Comprehension questions are embedded at natural break points within the article body, and continued reading is gated behind correct answers — creating a structured, paced reading experience rather than a passive scroll.

5. **Assignment customization is exceptionally granular.** The combination of activity selection (9 activity types), Lexile level locking, scheduling, and accessibility toggles (audio, translation, vocabulary AI) gives teachers fine-grained control over the student reading experience.

6. **Content breadth with editorial curation.** The Newsela library spans News, ELA literary texts, Social Studies, STEM, Spanish editions, SEL, LGBTQIA+ collections, videos, worksheets, and novel studies — with active editorial curation from subject-matter teams.

7. **Luna AI Search supports natural language queries.** Teachers can search with full sentences while getting clarifying questions and filtered results — a meaningfully different interaction model than keyword search with manual filter dropdowns.

8. **Reports map to actionable instructional decisions.** The 8-skill Reading Skills breakdown in the Classroom Data report gives teachers diagnostic data at the skill level, not just the score level — directly informing which ELA standards need reteaching.

## KNOWN LIMITATIONS

> **✓ NOTE** · These limitations were directly observed during hands-on exploration on March 23, 2026. Luna AI output quality was not stress-tested across multiple articles or input variations — limitations related to AI consistency, calibration, or hallucination are not included here and should be evaluated separately.

1. **Luna AI Search is still BETA.** The conversational search experience is promising but not yet polished — the clarifying question flow can feel disruptive when a teacher already knows what they want. A direct "search anyway" affordance would improve the experience.

2. **Assignment creation requires pre-configured classes.** New users see an empty "Students" field with a prompt to set up classes. The LMS sync path (Google Classroom, Clever, Canvas) is the primary onboarding vector, which may create friction for schools with non-standard LMS setups.

3. **Luna Lesson Planner panel takes significant screen real estate.** The 5-tab side panel expands into the article reading area, significantly reducing the visible text column. On smaller screens, this creates a cramped reading experience. The "Hide" button mitigates this, but panel state is not persistent across sessions.

4. **Article reader presents three simultaneous panels with no layout flexibility.** The article body, the Luna AI panel, and the Assignment Builder panel can all be open simultaneously, with no way to reposition or undock them. On Chromebooks and 11" laptops — common in K–12 classrooms — this leaves very little usable screen space for the actual text.

5. **Reports require class setup to populate.** The Classroom Data report shows meaningful data only when classes are synced and students are actively assigned content. For a new teacher account, the reports show N/A and empty tables.

6. **AI transparency disclaimers are present but small.** Every Luna-generated output includes a disclaimer noting AI involvement. This is appropriate, but the disclaimer text is small and italicized — may be missed by busy teachers.

7. **Power Word AI vocabulary supports can be disabled per assignment but not globally.** Teachers must remember to disable AI vocabulary supports on each new assignment if they prefer students to infer meaning independently. A global default preference setting would reduce this friction.
